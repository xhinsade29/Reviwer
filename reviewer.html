Information and Communication Technology (ICT) Reviewer

Definition

ICT = infrastructure + components enabling modern computing & communication.
Includes devices, networks, apps, and systems for digital interaction.

Uses of ICT in Daily Life

  1. Communication: Transition: letters ‚Üí phones ‚Üí internet-based tools. Examples: chat, email, voicemail, social media. Cheapest and fastest means of communication. Helps students: critical thinking, problem-solving, collaboration, access to remote learning.
  2. Job Opportunities: Essential in most careers; employers expect ICT skills. Automates tasks ‚Üí efficiency (e.g., robots in pharmacies). ICT frees employees for roles requiring human intelligence. Job searching now done online (smartphone, laptop, gadgets).
  3. Education: Teachers must be digitally literate to maximize ICT benefits. Tools: interactive boards, mobile learning, flipped classroom. Enhances creativity, higher-order thinking, and individualized learning. Prepares students for technological changes in society/work.
  4. Socializing: Social media revolutionized communication & networking. Benefits: maintaining connections, self-confidence, access to info. Platforms: Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, LinkedIn, blogs, wikis, etc. Internet = global interconnection of networks for info sharing. Socialization: adapting behaviors through ICT-supported learning.

Impact of ICT on Society

Positive Impacts
  • Access to vast info and services (cheaper communication: VoIP, IM).
  • Improved education (distance learning, e-learning, VR).
  • New tools (digital cameras, software, assistive tech for disabled).
  • Global communication & cost savings (email, video calls, e-commerce).
  • Better info management (data mining, stock control, cash flow).
Negative Impacts
  • Job loss (automation, outsourcing).
  • Reduced personal interaction ‚Üí social isolation.
  • Reduced physical activity ‚Üí health risks (obesity, heart disease).
  • High costs (hardware, software, maintenance, training).
  • Competition ‚Üí smaller orgs may struggle against bigger players.

Reviewer: Computer & Applications of ICT

Definition of Computer
  • A programmable machine.
  • An electronic device that stores, retrieves, and processes data.
  • Manipulates data according to instructions (programs).
  • Any device that aids humans in performing computations/calculations.
Principal Characteristics of Computers
  • Responds to a specific set of instructions in a defined way.
  • Executes pre-recorded list of instructions.
  • Quickly stores and retrieves large amounts of data.

Reviewer: Evolution of the Web & Types of Websites

üåê Web 1.0 (Read-Only Static Web)
  • Definition: First stage of the web; static, read-only, information portal.
  • Features: Company homepage focus, directories for organizing web, content positioned with tables.
  • Examples: Mp3.com, Homepages, Directories, Page Views, HTML portals.
  • Disadvantages: Read-only (no interaction), limited user participation, lack of standards.
üåê Web 2.0 (Read-Write Interactive Web)
  • Definition: Interactive, collaborative web focused on user-generated content.
  • Features: Users control & contribute data, dynamic pages, collaboration via blogs, wikis, social media.
  • Examples: Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter, Wordpress, Blogger, Tumblr, Wikipedia, YouTube, Vimeo.
  • Key Features: Folksonomy (user tagging), rich user interface, user participation, long tail (on-demand services).
üåê Web 3.0 (Read-Write Intelligent Web)
  • Definition: Intelligent, semantic web (John Markoff, NY Times).
  • Features: Connected, open, data-driven, semantic technologies, AI, machine learning, NLP, autonomous agents, personalized web experience.
  • Concepts: Semantic web = framework for sharing/reusing data, web of data (system-readable).
Types of Websites
  • eCommerce: Online shopping (Amazon, Lazada, Shopee).
  • Business: Company info, branding, services (corporate sites).
  • Entertainment: Fun content, media (Netflix, Buzzfeed).
  • Portfolio: Showcasing personal work (freelancers, designers).
  • Media: News & reporting (CNN, BBC).
  • Brochure: Simple info sites (basic small-business presence).
  • Nonprofit: Donations & causes (UNICEF, Red Cross).
  • Educational: School websites, online courses (Coursera, DepEd).
  • Infopreneur: Selling info products (ebooks, tutorials).
  • Personal: Blogs, vlogs, diaries.
  • Web Portal: Centralized access to info, often with login (Yahoo!, company portals).
  • Wiki/Forum: Collaborative editing & discussions (Wikipedia, Reddit, fandom wikis).
